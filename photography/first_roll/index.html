My First Roll

Everyone has to start somewhere, and for me it was here. After receiving my camera, I wasn't entirely sure where to begin, so I bought a roll of iso 400 film and went to the park. I spent a few hours experimenting with the lenses I'd inherited and trying my best to produce something decent.

Unfortunately, there were two key things I failed to realize in my endeavors:

  • While my camera had a light meter for determining proper exposure, I was not using it in conjunction with the camera's depth of field preview button. This resulted in metering that was inaccurate for many shots.
  • When using my zoom lens for the first time, I did not realize how significant the lack of OIS was (nor how shakey my hands were). This resulted in several blurry zoomed shots.

All of that being said, I'm not ashamed of any of these shots. They were the products of a necessary learning experience, and I appreciate them as such.
